Diabetic wound healing is a complex clinical challenge driven by systemic disruptions in protein interaction networks, leading to impaired angiogenesis, prolonged inflammation, and defective extracellular matrix remodeling. Despite advances in network biology, the application of AI-driven graph learning to model and contrast healthy versus diabetic protein interactomes remains limited. In this study, interaction data for twenty biologically relevant seed genes — spanning inflammatory cytokines (TNF, IL6, IL1B), growth factors (VEGFA, FGF2, TGFB1), matrix remodeling proteins (MMP9, COL1A1, TIMP1), and insulin-signaling mediators (INS, IGF1, AKT1) — were retrieved from the STRING database (confidence score ≥ 700) with network expansion to approximately 400 nodes. A healthy PPI network was constructed and a diabetic counterpart was simulated through biologically informed edge disruption, applying higher removal probabilities to seed-gene interactions and low-confidence edges, resulting in substantial connectivity loss. Degree centrality was computed across both networks to derive hub-shift scores for each protein, and the top disrupted hub proteins were identified. Node2Vec embeddings (128 dimensions, walk length 20, 100 walks) were generated from the healthy network and used as input features for a Graph Attention Network (GAT) trained via contrastive link-prediction over 100 epochs. Disruption scores were subsequently computed by comparing learned embeddings against diabetic neighbor representations, enabling therapeutic target prioritization. Functional enrichment analysis using g:Profiler mapped top disrupted proteins to pathways in angiogenesis, insulin signaling, and ECM remodeling. The GAT model achieved a ROC-AUC of 0.92, confirming strong discriminative power between true and spurious interactions. Hub-shift analysis revealed a marked collapse in central regulatory proteins under diabetic conditions, while enrichment results implicated HIF1A, VEGFA, and AKT1 as high-priority therapeutic candidates. These findings establish a reproducible, AI-integrated computational framework for identifying molecular vulnerabilities in diabetic wound healing and nominating actionable protein targets for therapeutic intervention.
Keywords: protein-protein interaction networks, diabetic wound healing, graph attention networks, hub-shift analysis, therapeutic target discover
